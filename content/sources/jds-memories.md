# Rememberances of Sturgis as Heard From John D Stoeckle

## Summary
Memoir-style recollections attributed to John D. Stoeckle. The document describes family life in Sturgis and references Seehafer family history and locations. A copy is stored at `content/sources/raw/JDS memories.docx`.

## Key Extracted Claims
- Unconfirmed: John D. Stoeckle states he and Janet were born in Highland Park, near Detroit.
- Unconfirmed: The family lived at 606 Laurel Ave in Sturgis for about 14 years, with earlier and later moves within Sturgis.
- Unconfirmed: The Seehafer family came from West Pomerania and immigrated in 1902, first to Minnesota and then to Ann Arbor.
- Unconfirmed: The Seehafer family had a house at 509 Cedar Street in Ann Arbor and Uncle Bill inherited it.
- Unconfirmed: Two Seehafer sisters were hospitalized at the state mental hospital in Ypsilanti; one death certificate (Bertha, 1960) listed schizophrenia.
