# Rememberances of the Seehafer Family as Heard From Mom on Trip to Poland

## Summary
Family reminiscence document focused on the Seehafer family, immigration, and later recollections, including a trip to Poland. A copy is stored at `content/sources/raw/REMEMBERANCES OF THE SEEHAFER FAMILY AS HEARD FROM MOM AND TRIP TO POLAND.docx`.

## Key Extracted Claims
- Unconfirmed: The Seehafer family traveled from Bremen, Germany to Baltimore, arriving September 18, 1902; only parents, Ida, and "Mom" were listed on the steamship document.
- Unconfirmed: The family first went to Minnesota near St. Paul, then moved to Ann Arbor, Michigan where the father set up a shop as a foundry man.
- Unconfirmed: Uncle Bill (Wilhelm Seehafer) was born April 29, 1888 in Germany and died April 13, 1958 in Ann Arbor.
- Unconfirmed: Ida died in 1948; two sisters were hospitalized at the Ypsilanti mental hospital.
- Unconfirmed: The grandparents Wilhelm (1921) and Wilhilmine (1922) died before the author was born.
